Choropleth map of U.S. Hispanic population by state

This map utilizes open data from the U.S. Census Bureau in order to map out the Hispanic population by state. This is displayed through a choropleth map, the darker the color, indicating a higher distribution. The map utilizes skills such as, thematic mapping, data classification, and basic map design to create a map for a general audience. Creating a visualization out of census data allows for this information to become more accessible.

Map showing the potential locations of healthy food stores in Philadelphia

This map is meant to show potential locations for a new healthy food store in Philadelphia. Based on a set criteria; within a Philadelphia empowerment zone, within 2000 feet of a subway station or regional rail station, and not within 1200 feet of a preexisting farmers market, or Healthy Choice Store. Utilizing ArcGIS Pro tools such as, buffer, dissolve, and overlay, multiple sets of data are converted into a visual representation. Allowing for this information to become accessible to those not familiar with data.

Map that shows the areas that are most at risk for drinking poisonous tap water

Multiple sets of data were used to pinpoint the areas that are most at risk for drinking poisonous tap water in Flint, Michigan. This map represents the cumulative knowledge that I had gained throughout the semester. A specific criteria was set to triangulate areas within Flint, Michigan where tap water quality was especially harmful to residents.Tools, such as, add/join, summarizing, and visual design were used to create this map. This is a useful visual representation of the effects of the Flint Water Crisis.
